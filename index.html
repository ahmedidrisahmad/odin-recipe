    SheklaTibs   Kitfo  Beyaynetu 

Description

Shekla tibs is one of the most popular dishes among Ethiopians.

It comes in a variety of forms, varying in type, size or shape of the cuts of meat used,
and can range from hot to mild or contain little to no vegetables. A particularly
recommended variation is shekla tibs, in which the strips of meat arrive at your table
roasting atop a clay pot stoked with hot coals dramatic and delicious.

Typically, the meat of the tibs that arrives at your table has just been cleaved from
carcasses hanging outside beside the restaurant’s entrance. Don’t be put off; meat
rarely comes fresher or tastier.

Ingredients

  • large red onion, about 2 cups, sliced thin
  • 1/4 cup niter kebbeh or ghee spiced butter
  • 2 pounds venison, lamb or beef, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • tablespoons berbere
  • 1 teaspoon ground fenugreek
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground clove
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 to 4 garlic cloves, sliced thinly
  • 2 cups whole peeled tomatoes, broken into bits
  • 1 to 5 green chiles, such as jalapenos or serranos

    INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Get the saute pan or wok very hot.
  2. Stir-fry the onions without the butter for a few minutes, until they char just a little on the outside.
  3. Add the spiced butter and the venison.
  4. Stir-fry hot and fast until the outside of the meat is brown but the inside of the meat is still very rare.
  5. You need to do this on as hot a burner as you have. Do it in two batches unless you have a very large wok or pan.
  6. The moment the meat has browned, add it all back into the pan along with the spices, garlic and chiles.
  7. Stir-fry another 30 seconds or so, then add the tomatoes and the wine. Toss to combine and let this cook for a minute or two. Serve at once with bread or injera.
